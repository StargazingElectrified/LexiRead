What is dyslexia? ▶
Dyslexia is a specific learning disability that affects reading, spelling, and writing. It is characterized by difficulty in recognizing and decoding words, and is not related to intelligence or effort.
What causes dyslexia? ▶
Dyslexia is believed to be caused by genetic and neurobiological factors. It affects how the brain processes written and spoken language, particularly in areas related to phonological awareness.
Can dyslexia be treated? ▶
While there is no cure for dyslexia, individuals can receive specialized interventions and accommodations that help them manage the condition and succeed academically and professionally.
How common is dyslexia? ▶
Dyslexia affects approximately 1 in 10 people worldwide, making it one of the most common learning disabilities. Early diagnosis and intervention can lead to significantly better outcomes.
What are the strengths of dyslexic thinkers? ▶
Many dyslexic individuals show exceptional strengths in creative thinking, problem-solving, spatial reasoning, and big-picture thinking. Many artists, entrepreneurs, and scientists identify as dyslexic.
